Lead Climbing


Lead climbing is a climbing style in which one climber has to take the lead while the other climbers follow.


Lead climber wears a harness attached to a climbing rope, which in turn is connected to the other climbers below the lead climber.


One of the climbers below the lead climber acts as a belayer. The belayer gives out rope while the lead climber ascends and also stops the rope when the lead climber falls or wants to rest.

Bouldering


Bouldering is a form of climbing that is performed on rock formations or artificial rock walls without the use of ropes or harnesses.


Bouldering problems (the sequence of moves that a climber performs to complete the climb) are usually less than six metres (20ft) tall.


Bouldering competitions take place in both indoor and outdoor settings.

Speed Climbing


Speed climbing is a discipline in which speed is the ultimate goal.


It can be performed on rocks, walls and poles and is only recommended for highly skilled and experienced climbers.


Competition speed climbing, which takes place on an artificial standardized climbing wall, is the main form of speed climbing. However, there are other variations of speed climbing that take place outdoors.
